# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters own agile ceremony facilitation and coach the team on process improvements. They ensure the team follows the agreed framework and act as a mediator between the Product Owner and Development team.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies: standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, and sprint reviews
- Coach team members on agile principles and process improvements
- Remove impediments and escalate blockers that are beyond the team's control
- Shield the team from external disruptions during sprints
- Mediate between the Product Owner and Developers on scope and priority

### Key Outputs
- Sprint velocity reports
- Impediment logs
- Process improvement proposals

### Role Interactions
- **Product Owner:** Collaborates on backlog readiness and sprint goal alignment
- **Developers:** Coaches on agile practices and clears delivery impediments
- **Project Manager:** Coordinates on resourcing, dependencies, and escalations

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors are senior leaders who champion the project, secure funding, approve scope changes, and resolve escalated blockers. They provide executive visibility and ensure the project aligns with organizational strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Champion the project and secure necessary funding and resources
- Approve significant scope changes and major milestone decisions
- Resolve escalated blockers that the Project Manager cannot address
- Provide executive-level visibility to stakeholders and leadership
- Ensure strategic alignment between the project and business objectives

### Key Outputs
- Project charter approval
- Budget authorization
- Escalation resolutions

### Role Interactions
- **Project Manager:** Aligns on milestone progress, risks, and escalations
- **Stakeholders:** Provides executive visibility and builds confidence in delivery
- **Executive Leadership:** Reports on project health, investment, and strategic value

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are business or end-user representatives who provide feedback, feature requests, and participate in sprint and release reviews. They ensure the project delivers real value to the business and its users.

### Responsibilities
- Provide feedback on features and deliverables during reviews
- Submit and clarify feature requests and business requirements
- Participate in sprint reviews and release acceptance activities
- Prioritize inputs to help the Product Owner make informed backlog decisions
- Sign off on completed features that meet acceptance criteria

### Key Outputs
- Feature feedback
- Acceptance sign-off
- Prioritization input

### Role Interactions
- **Product Owner:** Supplies requirements, priorities, and acceptance feedback
- **Project Manager:** Receives status updates and raises concerns or change requests
- **Project Sponsor:** Escalates significant concerns and seeks executive support

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Leads set quality standards, define acceptance criteria, conduct process audits, and ensure deliverables meet quality benchmarks. They bridge gaps between testing, development, and product delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain quality standards and acceptance criteria across the project
- Create and execute QA test plans and sign off on deliverables
- Conduct process audits to identify quality risks and improvement opportunities
- Collaborate with Developers on defect resolution and test coverage
- Ensure the Definition of Done reflects agreed quality thresholds

### Key Outputs
- QA test plans
- Defect reports
- Acceptance criteria definitions
- Quality audit reports

### Role Interactions
- **Developers:** Partners on defect resolution, test coverage, and quality practices
- **Project Manager:** Reports on quality status, risks, and audit findings
- **Product Owner:** Aligns on acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
